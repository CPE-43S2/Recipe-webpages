KINILAW NA TAMILOK

OVERVIEW

Tamilok is a unique Filipino delicacy that many people mistakenly call a "woodworm," but it's actually a marine mollusk - specifically a shipworm (Teredo navalis). This mollusk is a type of saltwater clam that burrows into submerged wooden structures like mangrove trees, which is why it gets the woodworm nickname.

In terms of culinary use, tamilok is most commonly prepared as "kinilaw" - a Filipino ceviche-style dish where it's marinated in vinegar and citrus juices (usually calamansi), which effectively "cooks" the mollusk through acid. When prepared properly, tamilok has a soft, creamy texture similar to oysters with a mild, slightly sweet flavor profile.


INGREDIENTS

  • 10-20 pcs fresh tamilok
  • 4 tbps finely chopped red onions
  • 1/2 tbps chopped siling labuyo
  • 4 tbps freshly squeezed calamansi juice
  • 1/4 cup white vinegar
  • pinch of salt
  • 1 tbps chopped cilantro or scallions

EQUIPMENT

  • sharp knife
  • glass bowl
  • citrus juicer
  • cutting board
  • measuring spoons

HOW TO MAKE?

  1. Clean the tamilok thorougly under cold water
  2. Remove the hard portion from the head of each tamilok piece
  3. Place the cleaned tamilok in the glass bowl
  4. Add the finely chopped onions and siling labuyo to the bowl
  5. Pour the freshly squeezed calamansi juice and vinegar over the mixture
  6. Mix well but gently to ensure all tamilok pieces are coated with the acid mixture
  7. Let it sit for 5 minutes until the tamilok turns opaque, indicating it's "cooked" by the acid
  8. Adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt if desired
  9. Garnish with cilantro or scallions if using
  10. Serve immediately as an appetizer with your favorite beverage
