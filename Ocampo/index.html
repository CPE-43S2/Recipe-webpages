Dinuguan (Filipino Pork Blood Stew)

A rich and savory traditional Filipino dish

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork belly (or combination of pork meat and innards), cut into small pieces
  • 1 cup fresh pig's blood
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 2-3 pieces long green chili (siling haba)
  • 2 cups water or pork broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat. Sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.
  2. Add the pork and cook until lightly browned.
  3. Pour in water or broth. Cover and simmer for 30–40 minutes or until the meat is tender.
  4. Add vinegar. Do not stir. Let it boil for a few minutes to cook off the acidity.
  5. Slowly pour in the pig's blood while stirring continuously to avoid curdling.
  6. Add the green chili and continue simmering for 10–15 minutes or until the sauce thickens.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot with steamed rice or puto (rice cake).
